The Reference System

Every shot above is generated from these. The face grid locks who he is, the body grid locks how he stands, the angles grid is the brand's eye, and the mood board is the world it all lives in. The prompts never describe the model or the kit — they only point here.

The rules that hold it together

  1. Never describe the face or the kit in words. Point at the reference and say 1:1, do not change anything.
  2. Call expressions and poses by grid cell number, never by adjective. Adjectives drift the face.
  3. No invented logos, sponsors, swooshes or federation crests. If it isn't in the product photo, it doesn't exist.
  4. Accessories never cover the crest, the name or the number. One to three per shot, from the props board.
  5. Every frame carries a vibe item — even the studio ones. Warm light always, never cold, never plastic.
  6. Validate on shots 1, 2, 4 and 5 before spending on the rest. If the face holds, the system is locked.
